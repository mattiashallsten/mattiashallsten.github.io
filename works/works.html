Breathing, bowing
Viola da gamba and suspended steel plate

In Breathing, bowing the tuning as well as fret positions of the viol are altered in order for the musician to be able to play intervals in seven-limit just intonation. As well as the viol, a transducer attached to a 100x60 cm suspended steel plate is used to play back the electronics part, consisting mostly of sine waves. The plate adds spectral information to the sine waves, and the pitches of the sine waves are carefully chosen to hit resonant frequencies of the plate.

Premiered at Nathan Milstein-salen, Kungl. Musikhögskolan 16/5 - 2022.

Play it Yourself
Interactive audio installation

Play it Yourself is an interactive installation where the visitors control a Yamaha Disklavier using their smartphones.

Technically, the installation is a node.js application running a HTTP server that in turn sends OSC messages to a Pure Data patch. The Pure Data patch then generates MIDI notes and control signals that it sends to the Disklavier.

All the code used in the installation is free and open source, and is avaiable on GitHub.

2018
fixed media
Walls of Amber
Ambisonics

Composed during the ambisonics course Nord+MIX in Vilnius.

This piece is composed in Ambisonics, with the intention of incorperating the spatialisation in the actual synthesis. Technically, this means that I use a SynthDef in SuperCollider that has spatialisation parameters, allowing me to sequence not only pitches and timbre but also spatial movement. This was partly a practical thing—I didn’t want to have to manually automate the panning in REAPER. But there is also an aesthetic intention, where each note is independent in its movement. Sequencing the spatialisation was also a way of introducing chance operations in the spatialisation, with the intention of moving away from the traditional acousmatic aesthetic of “grand gestures”.

2017
fixed media
The Aimless Blade of Science
Ambisonics

This piece utilizes a synthesis algorithm where a noise source is sent through a series of delay lines of varying, but short, delay times. The delay lines completes a feedback loop, and each delay line corresponds to one speaker. In a sense it is sort of like an expanded karplus-strong algorithm.

The piece was premiered in the 29-channel speaker dome at KMH, using 8 delay lines that were dispersed among the speakers. In the stereo version below the delay lines are converted to a stereo signal using the Splay object in SuperCollider.

2017

Musik för speldosor
Modified music boxes

In this work I purchased four identical music boxes containing the song “Happy Birthday”. I then removed some of the pegs on the music boxes, creating four distinct reductions of the melody. The four music boxes were later attached to a wooden box, with piezo microphones beneath each music box. The piece was then performed by four people, each one playing a different music box.

Premiered at Lilla Salen, Kung. Musikhögskolan 27/4 – 2017.
